Danny Masterson's Background and Career

Danny Masterson, born on March 13, 1976, in Long Island, New York, began his journey in the entertainment industry at a young age.

He started as a child model at the age of four and ventured into musicals and acting by the age of eight. Notably, he appeared in over 100 commercials for various brands before his sixteenth birthday.

Masterson's breakthrough came with his role as Steven Hyde in the hit sitcom "That '70s Show," where he featured in all 200 episodes from 1998 to 2006.

On top of that, he starred in "The Ranch" and pursued DJing under the alias 'DJ Mom Jeans.' His acting career also includes roles in "Beethoven's 2nd," the sitcom "Cybill," and the 2008 film "Yes Man" alongside Jim Carrey.

His partnership with Bijou Phillips extended to the 2009 film "The Bridge to Nowhere," followed by roles in "White Collar," "The Chicago 8," and a significant stint in "The Ranch" from 2016 to 2018.
